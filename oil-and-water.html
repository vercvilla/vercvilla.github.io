Oil & Water: The Case Against Solitary Confinement

COURSE

Advanced Graphic Design: Series and Systems

Yale University, Fall 2020

ASSIGNMENT

Design a booklet around three thematically linked articles.

DESCRIPTION

I designed an educational booklet about the negative psychological effects of solitary confinement. The title, "Oil & Water," refers to the inability of some prisoners to reintegrate into society after prolonged isolation. Set in a contemporary monoscript typeface, every character in the booklet is lined up and confined to its own “cell,” an expression of incarcerated rigidity in the modern day. Blurry titles are a visualization of mental deterioration. Thin page margins constrain the body text and create a slight sense of claustrophobia, a feeling many prisoners reported experiencing in solitary confinement. Blank spreads throughout the booklet symbolize how the experience of time expands in prolonged isolation. Coarse grey cardstock alludes to the stone and steel of prison, and the naked binding is reminiscent of metal bars.
