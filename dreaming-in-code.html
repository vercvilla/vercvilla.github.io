full paperback cover

I was tasked with using the original illustrations to age up this hardcover.

I wanted to connect 19th century mechanical computers with digital computers today.

Mechanical computers were programmed with punch cards like these. I used the circles to connect them with the LEDs and pixels of digital computers.

Dreaming in Code

An aged-up cover redesign for a biography of Ada Byron Lovelace, the world's first computer programmer.

The publisher asked me to target an older audience with the paperback edition of Dreaming in Code, and the author requested that I use her original illustrations from the hardcover. I aged up the cover by shrinking the illustrations and placing them against a dark background.

I used a dotted typeface for the title to connect Lovelace’s punch-card-programmed mechanical computer from the 1800s with the LED lights of digital computers today.
